Publications

Fluid Mechanics

  • Convection rolls and three-dimensional particle dynamics in merging solute streams

    Current

    Robben Migacz, Guillaume Durey, and Jesse Ault

    Under review, 2023

    10.48550/arXiv.2305.19929

    Presented at American Physical Society Division of Fluid Dynamics (APS DFD), November 2022

    We examine, by way of experiments, theory, and numerical simulations, the three-dimensional dynamics of fluid, solute, and particles in merging streams of distinct solute concentration. We demonstrate that the sense of solute concentration-dependent particle migration can change near charged surfaces as a result of diffusioosmotic flow, resulting in flow recirculation regions we call "convection rolls."

    I performed numerical simulations, analyzed experimental results, produced figures, and wrote the manuscript. Dr. Guillaume Durey performed experiments. Dr. Jesse Ault derived the theoretical solution for the dynamics and supported the project with funding and advising. We are grateful to Viet Sang Doan and Dr. Sangwoo Shin (University at Buffalo) for zeta potentiometry measurements used in this work. Part of this research was conducted using computational resources and services at the Center for Computation and Visualization, Brown University; travel to APS DFD was supported in part by a Conference Travel Fund award from the Brown University Graduate School.

  • Diffusiophoresis in a Taylor-dispersing solute

    Robben Migacz and Jesse Ault

    March 2022

    Physical Review Fluids

    10.1103/PhysRevFluids.7.034202

    Presented at American Physical Society Division of Fluid Dynamics (APS DFD), November 2021

    Presented at New England Complex Fluids (NECF) 86, March 2021

    We use theory and numerical simulations to describe the diffusiophoretic migration of colloidal particles in shear flows in two- and three-dimensional geometries. We comment on particle focusing at walls (or corners, in the three-dimensional case) or the center of the channel, depending on the species involved.

    I performed numerical simulations, produced figures, and wrote the manuscript. Dr. Jesse Ault helped develop the theoretical solution for the dynamics and supported the project with funding and advising. We are grateful to Dr. Bhargav Rallabandi (University of California, Riverside) for valuable discussions about this project. This work was funded in part by the Professor R. Bruce Lindsay Graduate Fellowship at Brown University; travel to APS DFD was supported in part by a Conference Travel Fund award from the Brown University Graduate School.

Technology

  • Arbiter: Dynamically limiting resource consumption on login nodes

    Dylan Gardner, Robben Migacz, and Brian Haymore

    July 2019

    Proceedings of the Practice and Experience in Advanced Research Computing

    10.1145/3332186.3333043

    Presented at Practice and Experience in Advanced Research Computing (PEARC), July 2019

    Presented as a poster at Rocky Mountain Advanced Computing Consortium (RMACC), May 2019

    Selected as Best Poster at RMACC

    We develop software to monitor the utilization of login nodes, which often serve as gateways to larger computational resources, and enforce usage policies. Users in high-performance computing environments can misunderstand the role of login nodes and run computationally intensive work that may affect the responsiveness or availability of shared resources. Our software serves as both an educational tool and a system for containing such intensive work by restricting the system resources that processes can access.

    Dylan Gardner and I developed the software and wrote the manuscript. I designed the poster and produced presentation materials; Dylan Gardner and I both presented them. Brian Haymore defined the problem and supported the project with advising. Funding for travel to PEARC was generously provided by the organizers of RMACC for the Best Poster distinction.
